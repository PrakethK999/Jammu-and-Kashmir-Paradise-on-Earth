Jammu, a region in the union territory of Jammu and Kashmir, is known as the "City of Temples" due to its abundance of Hindu shrines. It is a place deeply rooted in Hindu spirituality and is famous for the revered Vaishno Devi Temple. The culture of Jammu is a blend of religious devotion, vibrant festivals, traditional music and dance, and a cuisine that features a variety of flavorful dishes. Visitors can explore historical sites like the Raghunath Temple and the Amar Mahal Palace, while enjoying the pleasant climate of the region. Jammu is a destination where spirituality, history, and cultural heritage come together harmoniously.




Culture

Jammu, located in the union territory of Jammu and Kashmir, is known as the "City of Temples" due to its numerous Hindu temples. The city's culture blends spiritual devotion, traditional customs, and artistic expressions, with festivals like Navratri and Diwali featuring music, dance, and local artists.



Climate

Jammu, located in the southern region of the union territory of Jammu and Kashmir, has distinct seasons with hot, dry summers, moderate rainfall, and mild autumns. This subtropical climate attracts pilgrims and tourists seeking diverse weather experiences.



Places

Jammu, India's culturally rich region, known for Vaishno Devi Temple, Raghunath Temple, Amar Mahal Palace, and Bahu Fort, offers visitors royal heritage, serene spots like Bagh-e-Bahu Garden, and natural beauty like Mansar and Surinsar lakes.



Cuisine

Jammu's cuisine combines Dogra, Punjabi, and North Indian traditions, offering vegetarian and non-vegetarian options like Rajma Chawal, Chana Madra, and Dum Aloo. Local specialties include Kaladi cheese and Nadur Monje, while sheermal bread and sweet treats like Phirni and Patisa are enjoyed.
